Frequently Asked Questions
New to BNB Smart Chain testnets? Here are answers to common questions.
What is a testnet and why do I need it?▼
A testnet is a parallel blockchain network used for testing and development. It looks and works exactly like the BNB Smart Chain mainnet, but all tokens on the testnet have zero real-world value — you can't sell or transfer them for money. Developers and researchers use testnets to safely experiment with smart contracts and DApps without risking real funds.
Are these BEP-20 tokens real? Can I sell or trade them?▼
No — testnet tokens have absolutely no monetary value and cannot be transferred to the mainnet or sold on any exchange. They exist purely for testing purposes on BNB Smart Chain Testnet (Chain ID 97).
Why don't I see the tokens in my MetaMask wallet?▼
Two common reasons: (1) Make sure you're on the BNB Smart Chain Testnet network in MetaMask — not Ethereum or BNB mainnet. (2) MetaMask doesn't always auto-detect tokens. Use the «Mint» button in the token table to add each BEP-20 token to MetaMask.
How often can I request tokens?▼
Each wallet address can claim tokens once every 24 hours. After a successful claim, a countdown timer will appear showing when you can next request tokens.
Is it safe to paste my wallet address here?▼
Yes, completely. Your wallet address (starting with 0x) is public information. We only need it to send you tokens. We never ask for your private key or seed phrase. If anyone ever asks for those, it's a scam.
What is the gas fee and does it affect the total cost?▼
Gas is the computational fee paid to BNB Smart Chain validators for processing transactions. The total cost you pay is always a fixed 0.03 BNB — gas is already included in that flat fee and does not affect your final charge.
